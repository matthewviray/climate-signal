Why disappearing Arctic ice matters

Albedo feedback: Sea ice is bright white and reflects ~80% of incoming sunlight back to space. When it melts, the dark ocean absorbs ~94% of that energy instead — dramatically accelerating warming in a self-reinforcing loop. Ocean circulation: Freshwater from melting ice disrupts the Atlantic thermohaline circulation, which moderates climate across Europe and North America. Ecosystem collapse: Polar bears, seals, and entire food webs depend on sea ice for hunting, breeding, and survival. Under SSP5-8.5, the Arctic is projected to be ice-free in summer before 2050. Sea-level rise: While Arctic sea ice doesn't directly raise sea levels when it melts (it's already floating), its loss accelerates land-ice melt in Greenland, which does add to global sea levels.
